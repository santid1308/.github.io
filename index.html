our mission

To advance the understanding and conservation of Galapagos cetaceans by pioneering scientific research, fostering community engagement, and training future conservation leaders.

Through our work, we aim to protect the unique biodiversity of the Galapagos marine ecosystem, emphasizing the ecological significance of cetaceans and the need for evidence-based conservation measures.

Research Priorities

Spatiotemporal Occurrence and Species Diversity:

Important on understanding where and when different cetacean species are found in Galapagos waters, and the patterns of their distribution throughout the year. It includes the study of migration routes, seasonal movements, and the spatial distribution of species across various habitats.

Population and Health Assessment:

Focuses on estimating the size and structure of cetacean populations in the region, as well as assessing their overall health. It includes studying birth rates, mortality rates, morphometry, genetic diversity, and signs of disease or physical stress.

Social and Trophic Ecology:

Explores the social structure of cetacean population such as group dynamics, collective behaviour, communication, and social interactions, as well as their feeding strategies and ecological roles within the food web. It includes studying diet, foraging strategies, and interactions with other marine species.
